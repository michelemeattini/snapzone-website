# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

macOS power users — developers, designers, content creators, and knowledge workers who spend most of their day in front of a screen with multiple windows open. They are efficiency-obsessed, appreciate precision tooling, and have tried other window managers but want something that feels native, invisible, and smart. They discover SnapZone through word of mouth, Setapp, or macOS productivity communities.

## Product Purpose

SnapZone is a macOS window management daemon that runs invisibly in the background. It provides instant window snapping via drag gestures, configurable trackpad gestures, global keyboard shortcuts, a custom layout editor, an optional sidebar (picture-in-picture) mode, and real-time usage analytics that show how much time has been saved compared to manual window dragging. The goal is total workspace mastery — windows go exactly where you want, instantly, without breaking flow.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Magnetic. Invisible.

Voice: confident and laconic — the tool that does more by saying less. It doesn't explain itself; it performs. Not aggressive, not playful. Think Vercel for window managers.

Emotional goal: make the user feel like their desktop finally obeys them — that sense of a finely-tuned instrument in expert hands.

## Anti-references

- Raycast-style maximalist feature walls: avoid listing dozens of features in giant grids.
- Notion-style editorial-typographic coziness: no warm cream tones, no Playfair Display, no humanist softness.
- Windows-manager apps that feel utilitarian and gray (Magnet, Moom default look).
- Any beige-sand-warmth AI landing page cliché.
- Screensaver-style particle storm with no information hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Performance is aesthetics.** The site must feel as fast and precise as the app. Animations are purposeful, not decorative.
2. **Show, don't feature-list.** Visually demonstrate snapping, zones, and gestures — don't bullet them.
3. **Expert confidence.** Don't apologize for complexity. The audience respects depth.
4. **Invisible craft.** The best moments are the ones users feel but can't name. Transitions, spacing, and type should feel inevitable.
5. **One brand color does the work.** Don't diffuse. A single saturated cobalt carries the identity; the rest recedes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum, targeting AAA for body text.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support: all scroll-driven and entrance animations degrade to an instant static state.
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements.
- macOS-focused users, so primarily desktop; mobile is secondary but must not break.
